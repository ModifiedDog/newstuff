Effect Sizes

A Cohen’s d effect size of 0.20 (20% of a standard deviation) is sometimes considered a reasonable threshold for reporting small but substantively meaningful effects. Statistically significant changes are noted with an asterisk.

Cohen’s d is defined as follows:

\[ d = \frac{\bar{x}_{group1} - \bar{x}_{group0}}{sd_{pooled}} \]

Here:

  • \(\bar{x}_{group1}\) is the mean of group 1, often a treatment group.
  • \(\bar{x}_{group0}\) is the mean of group 0, often a control, or comparison group.
  • \(sd_{pooled}\) is the pooled standard deviation.
  • Thus, Cohen’s d is the difference between the means of the two groups, expressed in standard deviation units.
